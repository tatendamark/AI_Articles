    Artificial Intelligence will Transform Finance, Banking and Wall Street

    A report by IHS Markit predicts the global business value of artificial intelligence in finance will be $300 billion by 2030.

    Michael K. Spencer
    Apr 10 · 3 min read

    You probably didn’t know this but today, April 10th, 2019 banking execs are being grilled by Congress.

    As capitalism is blooming in 2019, where FANG stocks alone have gained $600 Billion since December, 2018 (5 months). The Banks are doing well too.

    It’s timely then to mention that a report by IHS Markit predicts the global business value of artificial intelligence in finance will be $300 billion by 2030.

    Fraud Detection and Mitigating Cybersecurity Risks

    Whether it’s detecting fraud or helping automate processes, there’s no denying AI has become more commercially viable in banking. Meanwhile, in Congress, the banking execs are all touting cybersecurity threats as a major and increasing issue.

    In 2018, the business value of AI was estimated to be $41.1bn, which includes the cost savings and efficiencies of introducing AI technology compared to keeping existing infrastructures and processes in banking, generally speaking. Now multiply AI’s impact by nearly 10 and you get to see quickly the influence AI will have on Wall Street in the decade ahead.

    In late 2018, a Fannie Mae survey of mortgage lenders found that 40% of mortgage banks have deployed AI — using it to automate the document-heavy application process, detect fraud, and predict a borrower’s likelihood of default.

    In the late 2020s AI Becomes Disruptive to Banking Jobs

    • More AI at banks will also lead to job losses or reassignments, with 1.3 million US workers expected to be impacted in 2030.
    • Asia Pacific is expected to surpass North America in the use of AI in banking by 2024.
    • Bloomberg, FactSet Research Systems and Thomson Reuters have all developed an array of data science tools and techniques — including machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing (NLP) — to quickly unearth valuable insights for thousands of financial professionals.

    Are Finance & Banking Employees Safe?

    Market experts have long debated how employees will be affected by increasingly introducing AI at banks. Some have suggested the new technology might not lead to massive job losses, but instead force firms to reconsider where and how to deploy their human capital.

    If AI is the automation of automation in the years to come in Finance, a great many jobs might be realistically lost and become outdated.

    Thanks to machine learning and artificial intelligence, investors and consumers are getting access to more innovative tools, new types of financial products and a new potential for growth. But it does come with a human cost?

    According to the Artificial Intelligence in Banking Report by IHS Markit, North America is tipped to be the biggest market for AI in banking between 2018 and 2023.

    Asia will lead in FinTech as China’s Economic Influence Grows

    By 2030, the Asia Pacific region for AI in banking is projected to reach $98.6bn. China will dominate FinTech, among other things, that’s a given.

    The next frontier: AI smart enough to help savers make good decisions about long-term buy-and-hold investments. So much for human investment managers.

    There will be better asset management algorithms. Asset management companies are increasingly relying on machine learning and big data analytics to make smarter, real-time investment decisions on behalf of their investors and clients.

    Outlook for AI’s impact in Banking, Finance and Wall Street is Good

    Bank of America Merrill Lynch and Morgan Stanley are among the bigger players in an emerging discipline known (awkwardly) as quantamental analysis. For many years, most jobs in the financial industry were considered irreplaceable by AI due to those jobs’ high-level critical thinking demands and complex nature. But now it’s estimated that 90,000 of the 300,000 current jobs in asset management will disappear by 2025, thanks to AI and automation.

    AI is also enabling financial institutions to create more personalized consumer products. 2020 is just the beginning of rapid change in the banking and financial sectors due to AI.
